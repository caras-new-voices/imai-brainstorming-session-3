Operating Plan · InfluencerMarketing.ai

How do we fix the
bottlenecks in the company?

A plan to unblock the flow from idea to revenue, give every stage a clear owner, and prove it in 100 days.

The thesis

We don't have a demand problem.
We have a bottleneck problem.

Revenue gets stuck at every handoff because we run the funnel as disconnected parts. And 80% of our future revenue should come from customers we already have.

The prize

Today we're a $2M company.
The plan is a $200M one.

100x doesn't come from working harder in the same clogged funnel. It comes from three compounding levers: capture the spend already flowing through us, grow the customers we already have, and win enterprise.

The diagnosis

Three bottlenecks. Everything rolls up into these.

  • 1We leave revenue on the table with existing customers.No expansion engine. Account management is reactive, not success based.
  • 2We can't convert enterprise.Products ship at 70% ready. No demand gen model. No single owner.
  • 3We're flying blind on data.It took four people to assemble one report. Decisions don't land coherently.
Where we start · the first lever

Get customers paying through the platform.

Today customers pay influencers outside our platform, so the money flows straight past us. Bringing that spend onto the platform is the fastest lever we have to grow.

  • The demand already existsWe don't need new customers to start. We just need to sit in the flow of funds that already moves today
  • Every payment becomes revenueA take rate on spend that runs through us turns one time deals into recurring, measurable income
  • It deepens the platformPayments raise switching cost and finally give us the transaction data we lack today
The answer, in one sentence

Give every department an owner. Give the company one map. Wire our channels into it.

Not one big bet. A system that makes the funnel we already have flow.

Part One

Ownership

One accountable name per department. Not a committee.

Six owners · one number each

Who owns what.

  • SalesGenerate + close new & expansion revenueBookings & win rate
  • MarketingDemand gen + the company & product narrative across every channelQualified pipeline + ROI
  • ProductRoadmap, the enterprise readiness bar, feature adoptionAdoption + readiness
  • Customer SuccessOnboarding, retention, expansion, advocacy & the feedback loopNRR + time to value
  • Data / RevOpsThe single source of truth: definitions, dashboards, attributionTrusted reporting
Decision rights

Who decides what.

  • Build / don't build→ Product, on a business case from Sales + Data
  • Can we sell it to enterprise?→ Product readiness gate. Sales cannot sell past it
  • What a metric means→ Data. No competing definitions
  • Messaging & channel mix→ Marketing. One brand story everywhere
  • Is an account healthy / ready to expand?→ Customer Success
The connective tissue

The weekly Revenue Council.

Thirty minutes. All six owners. The funnel dashboard and every feature in flight on one screen.

This is where the "chaotic strategic shifts" stop: every decision leaves the room with an owner, a comms plan, and a review date.

Part Two

The Feature to
Revenue Map

One request. Nine stages. A named owner and a gate at each.

An enterprise customer requests a feature

The baton never drops.

  • 1Intake & qualifyProduct
  • 2Plan & scope: revenue potential across accountsProduct
  • 3Build: must pass the enterprise readiness gateProduct
  • 4Launch: release plus go to marketProduct + Marketing
  • 5Measure: instrument, baseline, targetData
  • 6Onboard: drive to first valueCustomer Success
  • 7Retain: sustained usage & healthCustomer Success
  • 8Expand: roll to other brands & workspacesSales + CS
  • 9Track revenue: attribute it to the featureData + Finance
It's a loop, not a line

Feedback closes the circle.

Customer feedback (CS)→ Intake (Product)→ Build→ Launch→ Onboard onto the feature (CS)→ Expand↺

CS owns capturing what customers say. Product owns deciding what gets built. No request dies in an inbox; Product never guesses what enterprise needs.

Part Three

Channels &
Go to Market

Every channel does a specific job in the funnel, not a separate broadcast.

Each channel's job

One motion, five channels.

  • Platform (in app)Announce features where users already are, to drive adoptionProduct + CS
  • WebsiteNarrative + feature pages + proof + captureMarketing
  • SocialAmplify thought leadership, launches, customer proofMarketing
  • WebinarsEducate the middle of the funnel + nurture VIPs + generate demandMarketing
  • VIP close cycleExec briefings, private demos, roadmap previewsSales
When a feature launches (Stage 4)

The launch sequence fires in order.

  • 1Platform: announcement inside the product to existing users
  • 2Website: feature page live with the value story & proof
  • 3Social: launch thread + short demo clip
  • 4Webinar: "what's new & how to use it", driving demand + VIP nurture
  • 5VIP cycle: Sales books private demos off the webinar
The VIP close cycle

Webinars feed the cycle.

Identify target→ Webinar / exec briefing→ Private tailored demo→ Roadmap preview→ Close→ Onboard→ Advocate

One webinar series, recycled into social clips, website content, and VIP invitations. The engine that generates direct enterprise demand beyond Stagwell.

The Plan

The Next
100 Days

Three phases. Each ends with something that's measurably different.

Days 1 to 30 · Foundations

What we do

  • Kick off the drive to get customers paying influencers through our platform
  • Appoint the six DRIs
  • Stand up the weekly Revenue Council
  • Adopt one funnel definition + a metric per stage
  • Launch the data reliability audit

What's impacted

  • Spend that used to flow past us starts becoming revenue
  • Every department has one accountable name
  • One shared language for the funnel, so "what the number means" arguments end
  • Decisions leave the room with an owner & a review date
Days 31 to 60 · Build the operating tools

What we do

  • Make paying through the platform the default in onboarding and contracts
  • Publish the Feature to Revenue map; run one live request through it
  • Product: enterprise readiness gate v1, the pass/fail checklist (reliability, security, reporting, support, docs) a product must clear before we sell it to enterprise
  • CS: set the onboarding activation milestone (the measurable "first value" every new customer must reach). Accountability comes from it being CS's tracked number, activation % and time to value, reviewed weekly at the Revenue Council, plus a feedback loop with an SLA to route requests to Product
  • CS: success plan on 5 accounts; expansion map for top 10
  • Marketing: demand gen pilot designed; first webinar booked

What's impacted

  • Feature requests have an owner at every stage, so nothing dies in an inbox
  • No product reaches enterprise without clearing the bar
  • Onboarding measured by first value, not activity
  • Expansion becomes a plan, not an accident
Days 61 to 100 · Prove it in market

What we do

  • Report platform payment volume and the revenue it earns us
  • First feature runs the full launch sequence, platform → VIP
  • VIP close cycle runs on named enterprise targets
  • One revenue dashboard live; every stage has a number
  • First revenue attributed to a feature
  • First expansion deals from the top 10 map

What's impacted

  • One repeatable, measured GTM motion, not disconnected broadcasts
  • Enterprise pipeline generated directly, beyond Stagwell
  • Every bottleneck has a number trending
  • First advocacy assets (case study or reference) in motion
By Day 100 · before → after
Ownership floating between people→Six DRIs accountable
Features aren't prioritized by what customers need, and new features aren't effectively tested and shared with customers→A nine stage map with gates
Four people to assemble a report→One trusted dashboard
Onboarding tracked as activity→Measured by first value
Ad hoc, channel by channel launches→One repeatable GTM + VIP cycle
Customer feedback disappearing→A closed loop into the roadmap
The ask

Three decisions, today.

  1. Approve the six department owners.
  2. Approve a data governance audit to give us one trusted source of truth.
  3. Adopt one funnel definition + one metric per stage as company standard.

"Give me those three, and in 100 days every bottleneck has a number, an owner, and a trend toward $200M."
